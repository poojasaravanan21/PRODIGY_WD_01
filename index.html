A 40-Minute Meditation for Radiating Loving-Kindness

Begin by finding a comfortable posture, either sitting or lying down, where you can bring your awareness to your breath and body as a whole. Breathe deeply and rest here, establishing a stable platform of moment-to-moment awareness, riding on the waves of your breath.

Evoking Unconditional Love

Once you feel comfortable with the flow of your breathing, picture someone in your life who loves or loved you unconditionally. Evoke the qualities of their selfless love and kindness, allowing yourself to feel the warmth and radiance of their heartfelt embrace. Bathe in these feelings, accepting that you are unequivocally and unconditionally loved and accepted as you are. If you struggle to bring such a person to mind, imagine someone treating you with that same love and kindness. Visualize these feelings vividly, as this can be equally effective.

Even if you don't feel worthy or deserving, it doesn't matter. What matters is that you were or are loved, just as you are, for who you are now and perhaps always have been. Allow your heart to bask in these feelings, to be cradled and entrained in them. Let your heart be rocked moment by moment in the rhythmic beating of the loving heart of another. With each breath, let your heart be held and bathed in the warmth of this radiant, pulsing field of loving-kindness meditation.

If your mind starts to wander or you struggle, just notice it with curiosity. Gently refocus your attention back to the meditation.

If you struggle with focus, gently keep including yourself in the loving-kindness practice. Repeatedly return to the phrases, immersing yourself in their warmth. Stay present with each moment, embracing the feeling of kindness and extending it to yourself and others, effortlessly guiding your attention back as needed.

How to Meditate

Meditation is a practice that involves focusing your mind and eliminating distractions to achieve a state of mental clarity and emotional calm. Here's a simple guide to get you started:

1. Find a Quiet Place

Choose a location where you won't be disturbed. It could be a quiet room in your house, a garden, or any place that feels serene to you.

2. Get Comfortable

Sit or lie down in a comfortable position. You can sit cross-legged on the floor, in a chair with your feet flat on the ground, or lie down on a yoga mat. Keep your back straight but relaxed.

3. Focus on Your Breath

Pay attention to your breathing. Notice the sensation of air entering and leaving your nostrils, or the rise and fall of your chest or abdomen. Breathe naturally.

4. Observe Your Thoughts

As you meditate, thoughts will come and go. Acknowledge them without judgment and gently bring your focus back to your breath.

5. Set a Time Limit

If you're new to meditation, start with short sessions of 5-10 minutes and gradually increase the duration as you become more comfortable.

6. End Your Session Gently

When you're ready to end your meditation, slowly bring your awareness back to your surroundings. Open your eyes and take a few moments to sit quietly before resuming your activities.
